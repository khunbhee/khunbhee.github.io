Overview

Instrumental Masterclass

Instrument : Saxophone, Trumpet, Trombone, Piano, Electric Guitar, Bass, Drum set, Violin, Vocal

Jazz Theory and Improvisation

Introduction to Improvisation and Jazz Theory is a richly detailed course on fundamentals in jazz harmony/theory and improv/bebop. If you are new to jazz, this course is an excellent place to start. It will also serve as a very helpful and thorough review for those who need it. Topics covered include the following: Chord/Scale Review,12-Bar Blues,Song Forms,ii-V-I Chords,Bebop Line Construction,Target Notes And More

Jazz Combo

Students will have fun learning about performing in a jazz combo! They will get a chance to try their hand at drums or bass guitar, as well as jam on their own instrument! Explore basic improvisation, learn some new music, and become familiar with techniques for jazz performance.

Jazz Big Band

Students will recognize big band as the popular genre of music in the 1930s-40s. Students will learn the instrument families and sections of a typical jazz band and their roles within the ensemble

Audio Recording & Engineering

A typical session begins by preparing the room: choosing and positioning microphones, laying cables, and setting up tape or a digital program such as Pro Tools. Although live ensemble recordings are occasionally made in studio, the bulk of music recording is done into a sound board outfitted with as many as 96 channels, or tracks. The engineer records each instrument and part separately and often repeatedly, creating a number of tracks and takes to work with.

Jazz Competition

All contestants must be registered in the 2020 Thailand Jazz Workshop Complete the registration form and then click on ‘Competition’ to complete your Competition Application.

All competition entrants must send a copy of their country’s ID Card or Passport, a photo of you with your Instrument, and a short biography. The 2020 TJW Jazz Competition is open to all Instruments players, from any country. Rangsit University Conservatory of Music does not cover any costs of the contestant’s transportation, housing, or food for the Competition.

Awards

1st Place – 15,000 Baht

2nd Place – 10,000 Baht

3rd Place – 5,000 Baht.

The First Round

Upload your video to E-mail : rsumusic@rsu.ac.th The contestant will need to perform live with the band or backing track. The video must focus on the Improvisation. Having good video and more importantly, a good sound recording, will be to the contestant’s advantage. Deadline for uploading the video is 21 February 2020.

The Second Round

From the Internet videos, 10 contestants will be chosen to perform in the Semi-finals Competition...

The Semi-final Round

Semi-finalists will play one (1) selection of their choice, 5 minutes or less, with the Thailand Jazz Workshop Faculty Rhythm Section. Semi-finalist must have lead sheets for Piano, Bass and Drums, with clear form and arrangement, Intro-Head-Improvisation-Head-Ending. No rehearsal will take place. Contestants must have music, intros and endings, clearly marked.

Final Round

From the above Semi-Finals Competition, three Finalists will be chosen by the Adjudicators. The finalists will play a tune with 2020TJW Faculty Rhythm Section. Do bring the lead sheets for the rhythm section with clear arrangements. No rehearsal will take place.
